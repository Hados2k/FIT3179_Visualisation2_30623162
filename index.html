COUNTRY STATISTICS

When looking on the world scale, Australia given it's size
has quite a low death toll per year due to traffic incidents.
Juxtaposing that with the likes of China or India where
even given the population size of 1.42 and 1.38 billion respectively
have a very significant amount of deaths. These countries have
also actually seen a net increase in the
overall death toll comparing 1990 to 2019 statistics. It can be seen that countries like
Saudi Arabia, Pakistan and Nigeria have actually
seen a significant rise in annual death toll as a
result of traffic incidents, obviously as time increases
population size has also increased
yet other countries like Russia, Italy, Spain and other european countries
have actually seen a net decrease in the annual death toll
as a result of traffic incidents when
comparing them across the years 1990 to 2019.

AUSTRALIAN AGE GROUP AND TYPE OF ROAD USER

As shown, the greatest proportion of deaths per age group
is associated with 40-64 year olds
which can be explained as it contains the
greatest proportion of the population (24 Years).
Conversely, the smallest proportion of deaths is associated with
0-7 year olds which can be explained by the fact
that they cannot drive and therefore would be a
result of being in the vehicle during an accident.

Naturally, Drivers have the greatest number of deaths in terms
of road user as there is much higher count of
drivers than any of the other categories, Motor cyclists are
the second highest category which goes to
show how dangerous motor cycling can be, even
greater than a passenger in most years.

AUSTRALIAN MALE AND FEMALES - TRAFFIC DEATHS COMPARISON

Key factors explaining why Males have a greater proportion of Traffic related deaths

Vehicle Choices

Males are more likely to drive larger and more powerful vehicles which can be directly associated with a higher crash risk

Alcohol and Substance use

Males are more likely to drive under the influence of alcohol or other substances which significantly increases the risk of accidents occuring

Risk-Taking Behaviour

Studies show that Males on average tend to engage in riskier driving behaviours. This includes behaviours such as speeding, not using seat belts and aggressive driving

Occupation

Certain industries like construction and transportation have a higher proportion of male workers which are then more likely to be involved in a work related incident
